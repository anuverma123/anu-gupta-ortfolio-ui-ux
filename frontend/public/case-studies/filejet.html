The Problem

Compliance data existed.
Clarity didn't.

Users could view filing obligations but couldn't quickly identify what needed immediate action — and missing deadlines carries real financial and legal consequences.

01

Compliance status lacked urgency

The dashboard relied on a donut chart. Users had to interpret legends before knowing what required attention.

Critical tasks were not immediately visible
02

Frequent actions were hidden

Registering entities, creating groups, adding existing entities all required navigating through multiple screens.

Increased navigation effort, slower workflows
03

Task management lacked flexibility

No efficient filtering or segmentation for large compliance lists — finding specific filings was time-consuming.

Higher cognitive load, reduced efficiency
04

Navigation lacked context

The sidebar showed no pending work counts. Users opened each section just to check if action was required.

Reduced awareness, slower prioritization
05

Weak visual hierarchy

Overdue and completed tasks appeared visually similar, forcing users to read every row rather than scan.

Compliance risks could be overlooked

Key Design Decisions

Five decisions that
changed daily workflows.

Each decision was tied directly to a validated pain point — nothing was added for aesthetics alone.

1. Quick Actions Bar

A persistent action bar at the top of the dashboard surfaces the three most common workflows — New Group, New Entity Registration, Add Existing Entity — without any navigation.

Reduced navigation overhead

2. Separated Compliance Overview

Instead of one donut chart, compliance data is split into three independent panels: Open Tasks, Entity Registrations, and Order Status — each scannable at a glance.

Faster workload assessment

3. Advanced Table Filtering

The compliance table now supports Entity Search, Jurisdiction Filters, Task Filters, and Status Filters — enabling quick access to specific records across hundreds of entities.

Faster record discovery

4. Navigation Badges

Pending item counts are now visible directly in the sidebar for Registered Agent, Business Licenses, and Fictitious/Trade Name — no need to open each module to check.

Improved cross-module awareness

5. Status Hierarchy System

Status indicators were redesigned with distinct color-coded pills: Overdue, Urgent, Upcoming, Future, and Unacknowledged — giving instant visual differentiation by risk level.

Instant risk identification

Key Learnings

Enterprise dashboards
should guide decisions,
not just display data.

Information hierarchy

What's most urgent must be visually dominant — not equal to everything else on screen.

Task prioritization

Users shouldn't have to construct their own mental model of what matters most.

Workflow efficiency

Every extra navigation step compounds across hundreds of daily interactions.

Action-oriented design

The right question isn't "what data should I show?" but "what should users do next?"
